    The Problem

    Attendance shouldn't be guesswork

    Scribbled notes, forgotten marks, and no idea who's been absent three weeks in a row.

    Takes too long

    Marking attendance in a spreadsheet or notebook after every lesson. Tedious and easy to forget.

    No absence patterns

    You don't notice a student has missed four lessons until a parent asks why they're being billed.

    No parent visibility

    Parents don't know if their child was marked present or absent unless you tell them manually.

    The Solution

    Attendance built into your workflow

    Mark attendance from the daily register or directly on any lesson. Everything flows into reports and billing automatically.

    One-tap marking

    Present, absent, late, or cancelled — mark each student with a single tap from the daily register.

    Absence categorisation

    Record reasons like illness, holiday, or no-show. Add notes for context. Build a clear absence record.

    Parent notifications

    Automatically notify parents when their child is marked absent. They also see attendance in the portal.

    Attendance reports

    Per-student, per-teacher, and per-instrument trends. Spot patterns before they become problems.

    Batch attendance

    Mark attendance for group lessons or a full day in one go. Perfect for busy teaching days.

    Audit trail

    Every attendance mark is timestamped and linked to the teacher who recorded it. Full accountability.

    Frequently asked questions

    How do I mark attendance?
    Open the daily register or click into any lesson. Mark each student as present, absent, late, or cancelled with one tap.
    Can I record absence reasons?
    Yes. Choose from categories like illness, holiday, or no-show. Add free-text notes for additional context.
    Are parents notified of absences?
    You can configure automatic notifications when a student is marked absent. Parents see attendance in their portal too.
    Can I see attendance trends over time?
    Yes. Attendance reports show per-student, per-teacher, and per-instrument trends across any date range.
    Does attendance affect billing?
    It can. When generating invoices from the schedule, you choose whether to bill for attended lessons only or all scheduled lessons.
